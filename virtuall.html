Project Overview

VIRTUALL (Visually Impaired Research and Training Universal Accessibility Learning Lab) is a virtual reality application that enables students who are visually impaired to learn white cane navigation skills remotely under the guidance of an Orientation and Mobility (O&M) specialist or Teacher of the Visually Impaired (TVI). Students progress through increasingly challenging virtual environments, building the skills needed to confidently use a white cane in the real world.

The platform addresses a significant gap in accessibility training — limited access to O&M specialists and the difficulty of practicing across diverse real-world environments. The haptic white cane hardware connects via Bluetooth and is designed to feel and behave like an actual white cane, enabling a seamless transfer of skills from the virtual training environment to real-life navigation.

Interface Design

The instructor-facing interface was designed with accessibility as a core principle, featuring a screen reader toggle on every page. The wireframe covers four primary areas of the application:

Landing Page

  • Create a new account or log in with existing credentials
  • Screen reader toggle switch for accessibility needs
  • Links to learn about the haptic white cane and available training environments
  • Option to request additional information or support

Instructor Portal

  • Slide-through view of all enrolled students with photo, notes, and progress stats
  • Editable student profiles including cane type and session notes
  • Progress tracking across six skill areas: basic navigation, obstacle identification, obstacle avoidance, complex navigation, crosswalk, and stairs
  • Add existing students via join code or email; invite new students by name and email

Learning Session

  • Live video feed of the student's avatar in the VR training environment
  • Real-time session statistics: training time, obstacles hit, scenarios completed, and mastery %
  • Notes panel for the instructor to log observations during the session
  • Mute/unmute and end session controls; returns to instructor portal on session end

Training Environments

  • Progressive scenarios from no prior experience to independent navigation
  • Environments include figure-eight patterns, obstacle courses, crosswalks, and stairways
  • Designed for a seamless transition from virtual practice to real-world white cane use
